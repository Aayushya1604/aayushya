"confusing existence"

the story behind the pieces

i had quite a few themes i wanted to incorporate in my work for this project, but chose to go with the concept of our confusing existence.

creative process

many times we think who we are and what we do matters so much but in reality we are all collectively doing random things to fill time in our short lifespan. once I narrowed down on this idea, I began to create.

“lost and confused”

i wanted the image to capture a variety of personas as we would see in our everyday life. we have a confused baby, working dad, studious girl, hairstylist, gossiping aunt etc. these are the backstories i see when looking at the characters, but the audience is free to come up with their own, based on their experiences with people and life.

“confusing existence”

to mirror the confused bunch of characters, i personify the earth as also being a confused rock just floating around. it is surrounded by planets and stars. if you potentially zoom into their expressions, they are equally lost and confused. this further exemplifies the idea on a larger scale.

silkscreen prints

after designing the pieces with procreate, i translated these digital creations into physical art through the silkscreen process. this technique involves pushing inks through a fine mesh to bring my digital visions to life on paper.

recognition

my artwork was selected for the 2023 art & design juried undergraduate exhibition and was displayed in ringel gallery from february 20 to march 10, 2023. it was my first time having my work displayed, and it felt great to have my work on the gallery walls for my friends, faculty members, and fellow students to see. :)
